Turkish Kebab

Youll find kebabs in countries all over the world, so what does kebab even mean? Essentially its a way of lumping a number of varieties of meat dishes together. Most often theyre cooked either on a skewer or a spit. There is some evidence that the word itself derives from ancient Turkic languages (themselves partially derived from Arabic), and it meant originally "roasted meat."

Turkish Manti

A popular type of Turkish manti is known as Kayseri mantisi, a cultural marker of the Central Anatolian city Kayseri. Kayseri mantisi is tiny and served with yoghurt, melted butter (typically flavored with flavored with spearmint or Aleppo pepper) and topped with dry mint and Aleppo pepper flakes.Manti may be made from shredded meat of quail, chicken or goose in some regions of Turkey, while boş manti ("empty dumpling") lack filling entirely.

Turkish Breakfast

Turkish people love food and consider breakfast to be the most important meal of the day. For that reason, a classic Turkish breakfast, better known as “kahvalti,” is truly a sight to behold.
